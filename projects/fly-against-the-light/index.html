Fly Against the Light

A surrealist reflection inspired by the CHARLS survey
June 22, 2025 ¬∑ 2 min ¬∑ Hongchen Gao

Inspiration
China Health and Retirement Longitudinal Survey (CHARLS) is a large-scale interdisciplinary survey project jointly conducted by the National Institute for Development, Peking University, China Social Sciences Survey Center, and the Youth League Committee of Peking University. CHARLS aims to collect a high-quality nationally representative sample of Chinese residents ages 45 and older to serve the needs of scientific research on the elderly.

In this project, by borrowing from the surrealist style, I show the mental journey from rejection to acceptance to expectation in the practice of CHARLS.

This work shows the first feeling of coming to the village in the city. The keywords are crowded and disorderly. “Crowded” refers to the random construction of the houses, and “disorderly” refers to the random installation of electrical wires and pipes, giving people a dazzling feeling. This work interprets these two keywords through exaggerated expression techniques.

The first time I came here, the panopticism that Foucault mentioned in his book Discipline and Punish immediately appeared in front of my eyes.
